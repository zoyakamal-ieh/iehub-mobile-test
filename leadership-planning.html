Lessons in Leadership

A strong leader is the cornerstone of any effective team. True leadership in an ISoc is about serving your people and the Muslim community with humility and sincerity.

QUESTION

What do you expect from your team?

ANSWER

Prompt task completion, upholding Islamic values & consistent attendance. Empowering individuals with well-defined roles streamlines work and builds confidence. Don't burn out by doing everything yourself.

QUESTION

How do we improve our communication as Muslim leaders?

ANSWER

With effective communication techniques from the Sunnah. Learn how to convey the message of Islam clearly & compassionately, drawing inspiration from the Prophet's (SAW) methods.

Lead by Example

+

Serve your people and the Muslim community with humility and sincerity. Lead with genuine care and inspire through your actions, your team will naturally follow and respect your guidance.

Communicate with Clarity

+

Clear and consistent communication is vital for team cohesion. Ensure information flows freely, expectations are understood, and feedback is encouraged.

Defining Roles

+

Defining clear roles and reporting structures: everyone knows their responsibilities, who to report to, and where decisions are made.

Meeting Excellence

Focused • Efficient • Creative

To make meetings efficient, the host must ensure the meeting remains focused and the agenda is being followed. However, the goal is to make meetings enjoyable and creative, as opposed to being sterile and corporate.

3 Rules for Every Session

1

Create an Agenda

Prepare ideas beforehand and send the agenda out far in advance. Give a specified amount of time for each item.

PRO TIP:
Always start with an Islamic reminder.
2

Time is Essence

Lateness creates a culture of slack. Always start and finish on time. Allocate a timekeeper.

  • Decide on monthly or biweekly meetings.
  • Regularity increases commitment.
3

Action Points!

First meetings are for ground rules. Close every meeting with a "Thank You."

Never leave without knowing exactly who is responsible for what and the deadlines.

Building your committee

Knowing your role directly affects how well the society functions day to day. When roles are clear, tasks don’t get missed and decisions are made faster. It helps committee members know what they are responsible for, who to go to for support or approval, and when to step in or step back.

For in-depth information about each role, download our guide!

The Big Three

President

Overall responsibility for the society. Provides vision, chairs meetings, oversees the committee, and serves as the primary contact for the Students' Union.

Secretary

The administrative lead. Manages bookings, communication, and membership lists. Responsible for taking minutes and keeping the ISoc organized.

Treasurer

Financial guardian. Oversees the budget, manages reimbursements, ensures financial transparency, and handles fundraising and SU grant applications.

Supporting Roles

Media & Marketing

Manages the ISoc's external image. Responsible for branding, social media content, and promotional campaigns to engage the student body.

Events Lead

Delivers the ISoc's activity calendar. Manages event logistics, speaker requests, and ensures all activities provide value to the student body.

Committee FAQs

Click on a card to reveal the answer.

1

What is the single most important principle we must uphold?

Sincerity of Intention (Ikhlas). All actions should be done with pure intention for Allah's pleasure, turning work into worship.

2

How do we prevent the committee from burning out?

Regular check-ins. Have the vice team check in often and rotate event leads so stress is shared.

3

What is the biggest mistake a new committee makes?

Imbalance. Using all energy on freshers. Ensure regular events keep the society balanced year-round.

4

How should we resolve major disagreements?

Open communication. Arrange meetings to listen to all sides. Avoid suspicion and gossiping.

5

What is the Treasurer’s duty regarding Amanah?

A Sacred Trust. Finances are a trust from Allah. Handle funds with complete honesty.

6

We struggle to keep volunteers engaged. Solution?

Barakah & Thanks. Remind members of spiritual rewards and always give genuine public praise.
